menu verification

what is verification?

Verification is the process of analyzing restaurant menu items and determining whether any of the ingredients relate to the dietary restrictions that we support. Every listed restaurant menu has gone through at least one level of verification. There are two levels of our verification, both of which are described below.

verification statuses

When viewing a restaurant's menu on Honeycomb, you'll notice a verification status box - on the right hand side on desktop, and right below restaurant details on mobile. This status box lets you know which level of verification the restaurant's menu is currently at, which serves to give you a 'heads up' with regards to the level of ingredient accuracy to expect. While we strive to provide the most accurate data possible, there are distinct differences between "Active" and "Unverified" levels of verification.

active status

Active Verification means that we are continually working together with the respective restaurant to maintain an accurate database of menu item ingredient information. We make sure to stay updated with restaurants on either a weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly basis, depending on their menu edit frequency. This is not foolproof, but is definitely more accurate and reliable than Unverified Verification. We urge you to follow our Menu Disclaimer (see below) regardless.

Note: Active menu items have a blue hexagon.

unverified status

Unverified Verification means that we have used our menu-analysis technology to determine any problematic ingredients. Essentially, we have a virtual chef looking at every dish in order to determine what possible dietary conflicts there might be. It is important to note that we do not have any confirmation of accuracy from restaurants at this stage, which may result in a higher level of uncertainty in comparison to Active Verification. We urge you to follow our Menu Disclaimer (see below).

Note: Unverified menu items have a grey hexagon.

disabled status

When viewing a non-personalized version of a restaurant menu, you will see that verification has been disabled. This means that there is no 'matching' or analysis happening, and you simply seeing the plain menu as you would on the respective restaurant's website.

menu disclaimer

It is the end user's sole responsibility to confirm the applicable ingredients of any given menu item with the restaurant staff upon ordering. Honeycomb and it's listed restaurants are not responsible for any inaccurate menu items or cross contamination. Always remember to inform restaurant staff of your dietary restrictions and/or allergies.
